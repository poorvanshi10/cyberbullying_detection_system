Why This Matters

Cyberbullying transcends physical boundaries, turning what should be safe digital spaces into environments of constant anxiety. Unlike traditional bullying, online harassment is persistent, permanent, and often anonymous.

Victims frequently suffer from severe psychological distress, including depression, anxiety, low self-esteem, and in tragic cases, suicidal ideation. The anonymity of the internet often emboldens aggressors, making the harassment more vicious.

Automated detection systems are critical. By identifying toxic language early, we can alert moderators, warn users before they post, and ultimately cultivate a more empathetic and secure digital ecosystem.
